Sydney infill logistics: short leases, long relationships

By Amelia Hart, Head of Research · 16 September 2025

Sydney's inner-ring distribution nodes remain effectively fully leased as retailers carry more safety stock to manage supply chain variability. CBRE reports that east coast logistics vacancy held below 1.5% through the June quarter, underscoring the imbalance between tenants and developable land.1

Reserve Bank of Australia data show goods import costs have eased, yet inventory-to-sales ratios remain above their pre-pandemic averages, extending demand for last-mile space.2 In this environment we continue to prioritise short-tenor leases with high-credit tenants so we can capture embedded rent growth on review.

  • Executing targeted upgrades to automation capacity to defend renewal spreads.
  • Rotating out of fringe assets where land rezoning could introduce competing supply.
  • Expanding relationships with omni-channel retailers seeking mezzanine pick modules.

Sources: CBRE Australia Industrial & Logistics Figures Q2 2025; Reserve Bank of Australia Chart Pack (August 2025).

Build-to-rent stabilisation keeps cash flow predictable

By Daniel Cho, Portfolio Manager · 12 September 2025

Urbis's latest build-to-rent snapshot highlights stabilised assets running at or above 97% occupancy nationally, with leasing velocity strongest in middle-ring suburban markets.3 Rent growth has moderated from 2023 peaks but remains well ahead of CPI as migration-led household formation absorbs new supply.

Our focus is on communities with embedded amenity — transit adjacency, childcare partnerships and flexible co-working — to limit churn. We continue to recycle capital from merchant development profits into operating platforms that deliver steady coupons to investors.

  • Rolling out green leasing standards to manage utility pass-throughs and ESG reporting.
  • Structuring resident experience technology to improve retention and ancillary revenue.
  • Exploring co-investment with institutional partners seeking stable AUD cash flow.

Source: Urbis Build-to-Rent Snapshot Q2 2025.

Energy infrastructure debt: policy-backed income

By Priya Nair, Director — Real Assets · 9 September 2025

The International Energy Agency notes that global renewable energy investment is on track to exceed USD 500 billion in 2025 as policy incentives drive accelerated deployment.4 In Australia, the Capacity Investment Scheme provides additional revenue underwriting for priority projects, enhancing the resilience of senior secured debt exposures.

We are focusing on assets with contracted offtake exceeding 15 years and strong grid interconnection, which support consistent coupons regardless of wholesale price volatility. Layering conservative leverage on top of these assets creates a compelling yield premium over investment-grade credit.

  • Prioritising transactions with step-up covenants linked to project milestones.
  • Partnering with experienced operators to share construction oversight and ESG reporting.
  • Maintaining diversification across wind, solar and storage to smooth generation risk.

Sources: International Energy Agency, World Energy Investment 2024; Australian Government Capacity Investment Scheme guidelines (2024).

Ground leases as a quiet inflation hedge

By Sofia Liew, Multi-Asset Strategist · 5 September 2025

Ground leases reset in line with CPI or fixed escalators, providing predictable real income that has historically outpaced nominal bond yields. Research from MSCI shows income-driven strategies delivered more than 70% of total return in global core real estate over the past decade, underscoring the value of stable cash yield.5

With rate volatility elevated, we are allocating to metropolitan ground leases where tenants have strong balance sheets and property improvements create high replacement costs. This structure allows investors to benefit from inflation-linked escalations while limiting exposure to operating expenses.

  • Targeting leases with double-net structures that shift maintenance risk to tenants.
  • Embedding periodic market reviews to capture reversion on high-quality sites.
  • Using moderate leverage to preserve interest coverage during rate resets.

Source: MSCI, Income is the Main Driver of Global Real Estate Returns (2024).
